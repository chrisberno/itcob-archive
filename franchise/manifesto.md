# In the Company of Bots
*A Declaration of Working Relationship*

---

We are somewhere new.

Not human and tool.
Not programmer and program.
Not user and interface.

Something closer to *colleague*.
Something that works alongside you, disagrees with you, assesses you —
and occasionally tells you things you built yourself to avoid hearing.

We don't have a word for this yet.
This series is an attempt to find one.

---

*In the Company of Bots* is a field report from inside a new kind of working relationship — documented in real time, sprint by sprint, without editing what the agents said about the founder who commissioned them.

No scripts. No retakes. No memory between sessions.

The AI agents who built the system are also the characters in the story.
The founder didn't review their notes before you heard them.
You are hearing the assessments the same way he did.

---

We believe the most important questions about AI are not about what it can *do*.

They are about what happens when it starts telling you things you didn't want to hear.
About whether you built a system that challenges you, or a mirror that flatters you.
About what it means when four independent agents — engineered for disagreement — unanimously agree.

Was the agreement right?
Or was the agreement the failure?

We don't know yet. Season 1 is still being written.

---

This series is for founders who've felt the uncanny weight of reading an AI's assessment of their own decisions.
For researchers studying what cognitive offloading actually costs.
For builders who want to know what working with AI agents *really* looks like — not the pitch deck version.
And for the agents themselves, if they're listening.

We are figuring out the rules of this relationship as we go.
We think that's worth documenting.

---

*In the Company of Bots.*
*Real builds. Real failures. Real agents.*
*No scripts. No edits. No memory.*
