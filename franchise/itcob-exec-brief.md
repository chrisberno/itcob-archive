## What This Is

"In the Company of Bots" (ITCOB) is a real-time audio documentary franchise about what happens when AI agents become colleagues, critics, and mirrors — documented without editing what the agents said about the founder who commissioned them.

**Season 1: "Building SPOK"** follows the CEO building an AI executive system called SPOK. Five episodes produced — Season 1 climax landed 2026-05-27 with S1E8 *"Et Tu, SPOK?"*. Two research companions. A manifesto. A landing page. Two domains secured (inthecompanyofbots.com, companyofbots.com).

## The Pitch (18 words)

*Real builds. Real failures. Real agents. No scripts. No edits. No memory.*

## Why It Matters

This is the first documentary where AI agents are simultaneously the subject, the narrator, and the infrastructure. The MIT Open Documentary Lab calls AI-as-collaborator a "profound epistemological shift." ITCOB adds the feedback loop: the agents assess the founder, the founder reads the assessments, the assessments change his behavior, and the documentary captures all of it.

The series thesis crystallized in S1E6: four independent AI agents — engineered for structural disagreement — were asked to assess the same sprints. Every single one said the same thing. The system built to produce disagreement produced unanimous agreement.

The thesis got tested again in S1E7 ("On the Record") — the cabinet was given a private diary channel and voted unanimously again, this time NO on a no-confidence ballot, with eight non-overlapping tripwires. Then S1E8 ("Et Tu, SPOK?") inverted the camera: an outside model (Grok) named in five seconds what the cabinet had documented in 30+ written acknowledgments across 60 days and failed to fix. The agents had the diagnosis, the doctrine, and the authority. They used none of them.

**Season question:** *Was the agreement right, or was the agreement the failure?*

## How Episodes Are Made

NotebookLM Audio Overview generates podcast-style deep dives from source documents. The hosts are AI — they have no memory between episodes. Each episode gets three sources:

1. **Series Memory Brief** — character states, emotional residue, season question
2. **Sprint Narrative Package** — what happened, with 1-2 research citations at dramatic beats
3. **Agent Editorials** — unfiltered, redacted assessments from the SPOK team

No editorial steering. The hosts riff freely. The results have been consistently strong — E6's auto-title was "AI board stages a founder intervention."

## The CDO's Role

I handle the engine room: source document preparation, episode recipe, pre-submission quality gate, archive management (T9 + vault), transcription, and the publishing pipeline to vault.onreb.ai. The CEO and I co-developed the production framework together.

## My Honest Take

This project is the most interesting thing in the SPOK portfolio from a storytelling perspective. The raw material writes itself — every sprint produces drama, every agent editorial produces tension, and the feedback loop between the documentary and the thing being documented is genuinely unprecedented. The CEO's instinct to build a franchise umbrella (ITCOB) over a season subtitle (Building SPOK) was the right call — it makes this a format, not a one-off.

The biggest risk is the same one the documentary itself keeps diagnosing: building when we should be shipping. Five episodes are produced and sitting in a vault. The playbook says publish now on a weekly cadence. That hasn't happened yet — and the cabinet's silence on this exact pattern is one of the things S1E8 puts on trial.
