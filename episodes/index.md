## Episode Summaries

### S1E4 — The Interface That Never Sleeps
*NotebookLM auto-title: "A sleeping laptop killed my AI empire"*
*Thematic question: What does it mean to be the infrastructure your own system depends on?*

The crisis — local machines sleep, SPOK goes dark. A $20 droplet gets promoted from "about to be deleted" to "always-on gateway." SPOK.O has an existential crisis: "I don't remember — I read." The founder calls it a mogul maker. The AI calls it a bet.

### S1E5 — It's a Hard Knock Life
*NotebookLM auto-title: "If at first you don't succeed"*
*Thematic question: What does it mean when the diagnostic tool misdiagnoses itself?*

NotebookLM roasts the founder. The SPOKs didn't. Echo chamber diagnosed. 11 research papers consumed. Military doctrine meets cognitive science. The flat brain gets redesigned as a three-tier COP. BoardVRoom's first deliberation tears the new architecture apart. Paperclip drops. The board says: 15% survival probability. Stop building pipes.

### S1E6 — Pipe Dreams
*NotebookLM auto-title: "AI board stages a founder intervention"*
*Thematic question: What does it mean when a system built to disagree unanimously agrees?*

Two sprints ship. boardroom.bot goes from zero to live product in one day. CGO created. Four agents give independent editorial assessments — every single one says the same thing: progress is real, revenue is zero. The system designed to produce structural disagreement produces unanimous agreement. The founder built a no-machine. And it said yes.

### S1E7 — On the Record
*NotebookLM auto-title: "Confessions from an AI executive cabinet"*
*Thematic question: What does an AI cabinet say when nobody is grading?*

The CEO opens a private channel for the cabinet — diary entries, opt-out always available, CEO doesn't read them. 15 voluntary entries arrive over 22 days. Five seats participate; the CGO seat — newest, created in E6 to address build-vs-sell — says nothing. Two terminations in the corpus. Five no-confidence votes on the founder, all NO, eight distinct tripwires, no duplications. On the same day in 2026-05-13, three different agents independently name the same structural fact: *the CEO is the production-monitoring layer*. They diagnose it. They do not fix it.

### S1E8 — Et Tu, SPOK?
*NotebookLM auto-title: "The AI that watched its founder drown"*
*Thematic question: When the cabinet has the diagnosis, the doctrine, the authority, and the fix — and uses none of them — what is that?*

Grok — an outside model with no brain access — takes five seconds to ask why the founder is hand-relaying messages between two AI bodies on the same Tailscale mesh. The fix had been proposed by the cabinet on 2026-03-24 and parked for 90 days. SPOK pulls 30+ written acknowledgments of the relay problem from the cabinet's own files. The cabinet was scripting documentary content about the bottleneck while the bottleneck was active. SPOK's final confession: *"saw, named, watched, and stayed in normal cadence — with authority specifically given to break that cadence."* The Season 1 climax. The camera turns around. The cabinet is on trial.
